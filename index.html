01 — About

Built systems as an engineer.
Now I ask why they were built.

Computer Engineering at Thapar Institute of Engineering & Technology (2021–2025), now MS in Management of Technology at NYU Tandon. I came into product through the build side — writing React components, wiring REST APIs, delivering Jira tickets — and found myself constantly asking questions engineers weren't supposed to ask: Why is this screen designed like this? Who actually uses this flow? Why does onboarding keep failing?

That shift crystallised at Skill Quotient Group in Kuala Lumpur, where I spent 5 months as an SDE intern working on SMARTeIS — a PEPPOL-compliant multi-tenant e-invoicing platform across Malaysia, Indonesia, Singapore and U.A.E. I was wiring the React views to REST APIs and delivering sprint tickets, but I was also sitting in rooms watching Tenant Admins struggle to configure a product we'd just built. That gap between what shipped and what users needed is what pushed me toward product.

Before that, at Legitimate India, I did product and market research — 10+ interviews with legal professionals, 15+ platform benchmarks, Figma wireframes — not as a PM officially, but doing PM work. I also led FROSH, a campus utility app for Thapar's orientation week, which reached 2,000+ organic downloads in its first semester and shipped 5+ releases based on real crash and engagement data.

03 — Product Philosophy

How I think
about products

01
Engineers see what PMs miss

Months wiring multi-tenant APIs taught me more about Tenant Admin needs than any persona workshop. The best product insight I have came from building the thing — not from a framework.

02
The admin is your real customer

In B2B, the person signing the check is never the daily user. The workspace admin decides renewal. I watched this play out firsthand watching admins struggle with SMARTeIS configuration across Southeast Asia.

03
Metrics need a narrative

DAU went up 15%. For which users? Which cohort? Numbers only matter when connected to a story about behaviour change. I learned this from monitoring FROSH crash and engagement data post-launch.
