01 — Problem Framing

The two users
everyone ignores

When teams build B2B SaaS, they obsess over the end user who is the person doing the daily work. But multi-tenant products ship to entire organizations. That creates two hidden personas who make or break adoption, and most products treat them as afterthoughts.

"We built a great dashboard for finance teams. Then spent 3 months firefighting because workspace admins across 3 countries couldn't configure it correctly. The product was fine. The admin experience was broken."

Pain Point

The Tenant Admin is invisible

The person managing users, permissions, and configurations has zero visibility into what their team is actually doing. No health signals. No way to know if onboarding is succeeding or failing silently.

Opportunity

Fixing it = enterprise retention

In B2B SaaS, the Tenant Admin is your champion. They decide whether the tool gets rolled out to more teams, whether the contract renews, and whether they recommend you to peers.

Pain Point

Platform Operators fly blind

The team running the platform has no consolidated view. They can't see which organizations are struggling or where onboarding drops off across the tenant population.

Opportunity

Platform visibility = proactive CS

Give platform operators a tenant health dashboard and they transform from reactive (responding to support tickets) to proactive (intervening before a tenant churns).

03 — User Research

Three personas,
one broken system

Through building SMARTeIS — a PEPPOL-compliant e-invoicing platform spanning Malaysia, Indonesia, and Singapore where I mapped the three personas that every multi-tenant product serves, whether it acknowledges them or not.

👤
The End User
Finance Analyst · Daily User

Creates invoices, tracks submissions, manages approvals. Switches between ERP and the platform constantly because workflows aren't connected.

"I just need to create an invoice and know it was sent. Why does this take 7 steps?"
🔑
The Tenant Admin
IT Manager · Weekly User

Manages team access, configures regional tax rules, onboards new staff. Has no dashboard showing what's happening in their workspace.

"Three people submitted invoices to the wrong tax authority. How do I know who configured what?"
⚙️
Platform Operator
Product/Ops Team · Daily User

Manages all tenants. Knows nothing about individual tenant health. Learns about problems through support tickets and always too late.

"We found out an entire company's invoices were failing compliance checks when they threatened to cancel."

08 — Key Learnings

What building multi-tenant
products actually taught me

01

Compliance is a UX problem, not just a legal one

Working across Malaysia, Indonesia, and Singapore, I saw that different tax authorities, invoice formats, and submission deadlines create user confusion. Translate compliance constraints into clear, contextual UI moments so users don't hide them in error messages.

02

Your admin user IS your sales motion

In B2B SaaS, the Tenant Admin expands the contract, advocates internally, and decides whether to roll the product out to more teams. Every hour saved for a Tenant Admin is a direct input into expansion revenue.

03

Multi-tenant metrics need tenant level segmentation

Global DAU is meaningless when your product serves 50 organizations. A large, low-engagement tenant masks a small, highly-engaged one. Build tenant level health scores into your analytics from day one.

04

Customization vs. standardization is the hardest PM tradeoff

Every enterprise asks for custom workflows. Saying yes to everything creates an unmaintainable product. Find the 20% of customization that satisfies 80% of enterprise needs and let the rest be standardized and build configuration, not bespoke code, for everything else.
